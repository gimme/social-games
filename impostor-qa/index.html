❓

Impostor Q&A

4+ players

A talking version of Impostor. Everyone answers the same question out loud — except the impostor, who was secretly asked a different question with a similar-sounding answer. Spot who’s off.

🎯 The goal

  • Crew: you all got the same question. Answer honestly, then sniff out the one person who didn’t.
  • Impostor: you got a different question. Make your answer plausible enough to pass — without knowing what everyone else was really asked.

📝 Setup

  1. Pick a question-master, or let a phone deal the questions.
  2. Secretly, one player is the impostor and gets a different question that leads to a similar kind of answer.
  3. Example — real: “How big is an elephant?” / impostor: “How big is a house?” Both answers are “big”, so the impostor doesn’t instantly stand out.

🗣️ Play it out

  • Reveal the real question to everyone — now the crew knows what was asked, but no one yet knows who the impostor is, not even the impostor for certain.
  • Go round and have everyone give their answer.
  • Discuss, then on a count of three point at the odd one out. The impostor’s vote doesn’t count.

🎲 Twists

Any number of impostors
Rarely, there could be none, several, or even everyone — so a lone odd answer might just be honest.
Fuzzy questions
Draw the real and fake question at random from a group of similar ones, so the gap is harder to read.
Forced lie
A yes/no question where the impostor gets the same question but must answer falsely.

💡 Tips

  • Vague answers feel safe, which is exactly why they’re suspicious. The real tell is an answer that fits a slightly different question.
  • Good pairs share an answer but not a reason — keep a few up your sleeve.
