How to play

Impostor is a pass-the-phone party game. One phone goes around the table — the app only handles the secret bits, the rest happens out loud between you.

🎯 The goal

  • Crew: everyone shares one secret word. Sniff out the impostor who doesn’t know it.
  • Impostor: you don’t get the word. Blend in — and if you work out what it is, you win.

📝 Build the word pool

  1. Pick the number of players (3–12) and, optionally, a category like “Movies”.
  2. Pass the phone around. Each player secretly types a few words into one shared pool — nobody sees who added what.

🤫 See your word

  • Pass the phone again. Each player privately taps to reveal their own screen.
  • Everyone sees the same secret word — except the impostor, who is simply told they’re the impostor, with no word.

🗣️ Play it out

  • The app says who starts. Going round the circle twice, each player says one word as a clue — two clues each.
  • A good clue proves you know the word without giving it away outright.
  • Then discuss, vote on who’s faking, and tap to reveal the answer.
  • Watch out: if the impostor guesses the secret word, they win.

🎲 Possible twists

The host can switch these on under Advanced settings and set how often they happen — so expect the unexpected.

No impostor
Everyone shares the word. Sometimes nobody is faking at all.
Everyone’s the impostor
There’s no shared word — every single player is on their own.
Two impostors
Two fakers instead of one (needs 4+ players).
Decoy round
The impostor is secretly handed a different word and isn’t told they’re the impostor — they may not even realise.

💡 Good to know

  • Roles are random every round. The person who typed the secret word can still be the impostor — nothing links a word to whoever entered it.
  • The app tries not to repeat the secret word: it steers away from recently-used ones, remembering roughly half your pool. A bigger pool means fewer repeats; with only a couple of words it’ll cycle quickly.
  • Enter words everyone at the table would recognise — clues are no fun if half the group has never heard of it.
