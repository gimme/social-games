How to play

Impostor is a pass-the-phone party game. One phone goes around the table — the app only handles the secret bits, the rest happens out loud between you.

🎯 The goal

  • Crew: everyone shares one secret word. Sniff out the impostor who doesn’t know it.
  • Impostor: you don’t get the word. Blend in so the vote lands on someone else — or, if you’re caught, name the word to steal the win.

📝 Build the word pool

  1. Pick the number of players (3–12) and, optionally, a category like “Movies”.
  2. Pass the phone around. Each player secretly types a few words into one shared pool — nobody sees who added what.

🤫 See your word

  • Pass the phone again. Each player privately taps to reveal their own screen.
  • Everyone sees the same secret word — except the impostor, who is simply told they’re the impostor, with no word.

🗣️ Play it out

  • The app says who starts. Going round the circle twice, each player says one word as a clue — two clues each.
  • Then discuss and vote on who’s faking.
  • Miss the impostor and they win. Catch them and they get one last chance to guess the secret word — nail it and they steal the win anyway.
  • Tap to reveal the answer and see how it played out.

💡 Tips

  • A good clue proves you know the word without giving it away — too obvious and you hand it to the impostor; too vague and you look like one.
  • Enter words everyone at the table would recognise — clues are no fun if half the group has never heard of it.
  • As the impostor, vague is good cover — the crew are keeping their own clues careful so they don’t hand you the word, so playing it safe blends right in. Hide behind that, and listen — their clues might give the secret away.

🎲 Possible twists

The host can switch these on under Advanced settings and set how often they happen — so expect the unexpected.

No impostor
Everyone shares the word. Sometimes nobody is faking at all.
Everyone’s the impostor
There’s no shared word — every single player is on their own.
Two impostors
Two fakers instead of one (needs 4+ players).
Decoy round
The impostor is secretly handed a different word and isn’t told they’re the impostor — they may not even realise.

ℹ️ Good to know

  • Roles are random every round, with one fairness rule: the secret word is always one a crewmate entered — so the words you add never help you as the impostor.
  • Words get used up. Each round removes the word(s) it used — the real word and any decoys — so they won’t come up again. Twists like decoys and extra impostors need spare words, so they get rarer as the pool runs low. Tap Add words any time to top it up.
