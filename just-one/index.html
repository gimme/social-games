How to play

💬

Just One

3+ players

One player guesses a secret word from one-word clues — but any two players who write the same clue cancel each other out. Cooperative, and surprisingly tense.

🎯 The goal

  • Get the guesser to say the secret word.
  • The catch: duplicate clues are removed before the guesser sees them, so being too obvious can backfire.

📝 Setup

  1. Choose a guesser. They look away while everyone else sees the secret word.
  2. Each clue-giver secretly writes one single word as a clue.

🗣️ Play it out

  • Compare clues privately. Any identical (or essentially identical) clues are crossed out — the guesser never sees them.
  • Reveal the surviving clues. The guesser gets one guess.
  • Play through a deck of words and count how many the group gets right.

🎲 Variants

Say it out loud
Everyone says their one-word clue at the same time on a count of three. Matching clues still cancel — listen fast.
Too many cooks
In a big group, a deliberate duplicate can be a tactic to cancel an unhelpful clue.

💡 Tips

  • Aim for a clue no one else will think of but that still points clearly at the word — the obvious clue is the one most likely to vanish.
  • Pick words everyone will know; obscure words make for lonely, unhelpful clues.
