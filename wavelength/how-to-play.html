How to play

Wavelength is a pass-the-phone party game. One phone goes around the table — the app holds the hidden target and the dial, the rest is you trying to read each other’s minds out loud.

🎯 The goal

  • Each round one player — the Psychic — sees a target hidden on a dial between two opposites (say, Cold ↔ Hot).
  • They give a one-line clue, and their team swings the needle to guess where it is. Land on the bullseye for the most points.

📝 Setup

  1. Either way you need at least two people. Pick the number of teams (1–6). One team — the default — is co-op, the whole table playing together for a shared score.
  2. With two or more teams, choose the score to play to. Co-op has no target — you just keep playing for as long as you like.
  3. Tap Start game.

🔮 Give the clue

  • Pass the phone to the round’s Psychic and tap to reveal.
  • Only the Psychic sees the spectrum and the hidden target wedge. Think of a clue that points to exactly that spot — not too extreme, not too safe.
  • Say it out loud — that's the whole clue — then hide the target and hand the phone over.

🎚️ Make the guess

  • The Psychic’s team talks it over and drags the needle to one of the 21 marks — it snaps and buzzes as it crosses each one — then locks in.
  • The target appears and you score by where the needle landed.

🏆 Scoring

The hidden wedge has five bands. The closer to the centre, the more points — exactly like the real Wavelength device.

4 — Bullseye
The needle sits on the blue centre band.
3 — So close
Either red band next to the centre.
2 — On the board
The orange bands at the edge of the wedge.
0 — Missed
Anywhere off the wedge.

🔁 Winning

  • With teams, play passes to the next team each round; the first to the target score wins.
  • Co-op (one team) just keeps playing — pile up as big a shared score as you like, then tap End game when you’re done.

💡 Tips

  • As Psychic, calibrate to the whole scale — save your most extreme clues for genuine ends, or your team will overshoot.
  • A clue that splits the room is often perfect: if half would go left and half right, the truth is probably right in the middle.
  • Guessers: argue it out. The conversation is the game — the phone just keeps the target honest.
